Summer 2016

Last Summer I took a break from my CS studies to spend 2 months in Beijing, China, at Princeton's intensive Princeton-in-Beijing program. For the duration of the program I spoke only Mandarin Chinese, and learned an entire year's worth over the course of 2 short months. In my spare time I continued to work on my various side projects, including groupme-utils.

2015-2016

In Fall 2015 I began my first semester at Princeton University. In addition to my CS classes and side projects, Several friends and I went to various, programming competitions under the team name "Team'); DROP TABLE teams;--". We won 1st place at a Jane Street hackathon at Princeton (eth1) in the fall, as well The Google Games in the Spring.

2014-2015

After the conclusion of my internship, I was offered a part-time position at GraySail, which I accepted. I continued to work for them part-time throughout my senior year of high school, and full-time the summer after. By the time I left for college, I had been an employee of GraySail for 15 months total.

Late Summer 2014

Near the end of the summer, I went to Indianapolis, IN to compete in the BDPA National High School Programming Competition. Our team of 5 won 2nd place nationally.

Summer 2014

The summer after my Junior year I interned for a local software company called GraySail. At first I spent my time learning their toolkits and investigating potential new tools for them, such as TypeScript. After a few weeks I started contributing to their main projects, including a research saftey management system (lab inspection/radiation monitoring software) for a nearby university.

2013-2014

My high school had no real Computer Science courses, but during my Junior year of high school I self-studied the AP Computer Science Curriculum. This served both as my introduction to Java, and a more rigorous introduction to Object-Oriented programming.

2012-2013

During my 10th grade year I was introduced to codecademy.com. My best friend and I had a competition to see who could earn the most points (earned by completing courses), and in the process I learned a lot more about web development, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Ruby, and PHP, among many others.

Summer 2012

After a friend of my Mom's gave me her 15-year-old desktop, I decided to try to fix it and put Linux on it. I learned a lot about hardware that summer. This led to me eventually converting my basement into a workshop, where I earned my spending money for the next few years fixing other people's computers.

Spring/Summer 2012

Near the end of 9th grade I started taking weekend programming classes with an organization called BDPA. My first year we learned ASP.net and Visual Basic, but in the subsequent years of the program we switched to PHP, in addition to HTML, CSS, JavaScript, SQL, and many other web development/general programming topics.

Spring 2012

In early spring 2012 I attended POSSCON, the Palmetto Open Source Software Conference. This was my first time hearing about many Open-Source projects, including Git, and most importantly, Linux. At the suggestion of several developers I met at the conference, when I went home I began experimenting with Ubuntu Linux on my home computer. I would continue to experiment with various Linux distros, and use it to this day.

Fall 2011

While learning how to factor quadratic equations in my 9th grade math class, we often had very repetitive homework assignments, so I devised a simple ActionScript-based program to solve the factoring programs for me. I enjoyed writing my first useful program so much that I continued to add features long after my math class moved on from factoring problems. I started teaching myself Python 3 to more easily write other, more generally useful programs.

2010-2011

It was around this time that I decided that I wanted to be a programmer when I grew up, and in my free time started to research various programming languages. I experimented with several, including Liberty BASIC, before finally settling on ActionScript 2.

Summer 2009

The summer before 7th grade, a friend of mine introduced me to Scratch, a graphical beginner programming language aimed for people our age. Over the summer and for several years to come, we made Scratch-based games for each other to play.
